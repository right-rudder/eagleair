## Private Pilot Certificate — Your First Step into Aviation

Flying begins with a solid foundation. The **Private Pilot Certificate (PPC)** program at **Eagle Air Flight Academy** is designed to introduce students to aviation through **structured, safety-focused, and personalized flight training**.

Whether your goal is to fly for personal enjoyment or to continue toward advanced ratings, Eagle Air provides the skills, confidence, and aeronautical knowledge needed to become a competent and responsible pilot.

---

## What's Included — Clear, Flexible Training

Eagle Air Flight Academy operates under **FAA Part 61**, allowing training to be tailored to each student’s schedule, pace, and learning style.

### Flight Training

- **Minimum 40 flight hours required by the FAA**
  - Most students complete training in **60–70 hours**
- Dual instruction with a Certified Flight Instructor (CFI)
- Solo flight training, including supervised solo cross-country flights
- Local and cross-country navigation training
- Day and night flight experience
- Maneuvers, takeoffs, landings, and emergency procedures

### Ground Instruction

- One-on-one ground instruction with your instructor
- Aerodynamics, aircraft systems, and performance
- FAA regulations and airspace
- Weather theory and decision-making
- Navigation, flight planning, and risk management
- Practical test (checkride) preparation

### Aircraft & Training Environment

- Single-engine training aircraft appropriate for primary instruction
- Emphasis on checklist usage, cockpit discipline, and safe operations
- Real-world flying experience in local and regional airspace

## Training Progression — What to Expect

### Stage 1: Introduction & Pre-Solo

- Aircraft familiarization and preflight procedures
- Basic maneuvers: climbs, turns, descents
- Airport operations and radio communications
- Takeoffs, landings, and traffic patterns
- Emergency procedures and basic aerodynamics

**Milestone:** First solo flight

---

### Stage 2: Solo & Skill Development

- Solo pattern work and practice
- Advanced maneuvers and precision landings
- Ground reference maneuvers
- Night flight training
- Cross-country planning and navigation basics

**Milestone:** Solo cross-country flights

---

### Stage 3: Cross-Country & Checkride Prep

- Long cross-country flight per FAA requirements
- Weather analysis and risk management
- Practical test standards (ACS) review
- Oral exam preparation and mock checkrides
- Final instructor endorsements

**Milestone:** FAA knowledge exam completion

---

### Stage 4: Practical Test

- Final flight review
- FAA oral and flight examination with a Designated Pilot Examiner

**Outcome:** FAA Private Pilot Certificate

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How long does it take to earn a Private Pilot Certificate?**

**A:** Training time varies by schedule and consistency. Most students complete training in **3–6 months** when flying regularly.

**Q: How many flight hours will I need?**

**A:** The FAA minimum is **40 hours**, but most students require **60–70 hours** to be fully prepared.

**Q: Can I train part-time?**

**A:** Yes. Eagle Air is well-suited for part-time students with flexible availability.

**Q: What medical certificate is required?**

**A:** A **3rd Class FAA Medical Certificate** is required before solo flight.

**Q: Do I need prior experience?**

**A:** No prior flight experience is required.
